Assistant Professor · Data Science

Bridging Rigorous Mathematics and Applied Machine Learning

I am an Assistant Professor of Data Science in the Department of Mathematics & Statistics at San José State University. My research spans two interconnected areas: the nonlinear analysis of PDEs and chemotaxis models, and applied machine learning — including deep learning, NLP, and computer vision — with applications in biomedical imaging, climate science, and blockchain systems.

Before joining SJSU, I was a Data Science Postdoctoral Fellow at The Data Institute, University of San Francisco (2019–2021), collaborating with Harvard Center for Systems Biology and MedStar Georgetown University Hospital on GAN-based microscopy and clinical NLP pipelines. I hold a Ph.D. in Mathematics from Auburn University, advised by Prof. Wenxian Shen.
